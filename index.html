About Us

The Willow is a non-profit organization dedicated to empowering elderly individuals (ages 45-65) to create professional CVs, apply for jobs, and find valuable resources to enhance their career opportunities. We believe that age should never be a barrier to professional growth, and we strive to provide the support and tools that our community needs to succeed in today’s job market.

In South Africa, many older adults face significant challenges when trying to re-enter the workforce. Through our services, we aim to close the gap by offering personalized assistance, training, and resources tailored to the unique needs of the elderly population.

Government Programs for Elderly Employment

The South African government offers several programs that assist elderly individuals in finding meaningful work. These include tax incentives for businesses that hire older workers, as well as job retraining programs aimed at improving skills for modern job markets.

Statistics on Elderly Employment

According to the latest statistics, South Africa has over 6 million people over the age of 50, but less than 15% are actively employed. The unemployment rate for older adults is significantly higher compared to younger demographics.

  • 6 million+ people aged 50+ in South Africa
  • 15% employment rate for individuals aged 50+
  • 50% of older adults are interested in returning to work, but lack the necessary resources or guidance

How to Apply for Jobs

We are here to guide you through the process of applying for jobs online, whether it's through LinkedIn or directly via company websites.

Steps to Apply on LinkedIn:

  1. Create a LinkedIn Profile: Ensure that your profile is complete with a professional photo, summary, work experience, and skills. Highlight your achievements and make sure to add your updated CV in the "Featured" section.
  2. Search for Jobs: Use LinkedIn's "Job Search" tool to find relevant job openings. You can filter results based on location, industry, and job title.
  3. Customize Your Application: When applying for a job, tailor your resume and cover letter to the specific role. Mention how your experience and skills align with the position.
  4. Connect with Recruiters: If possible, connect with the recruiter or hiring manager on LinkedIn. Sending a polite and personalized connection request can help increase your visibility.
  5. Follow Up: After applying, follow up with a message to the hiring manager if you haven’t heard back within a week. LinkedIn has an in-built messaging system that makes it easy to communicate.

Steps to Apply Directly on Company Websites:

  1. Visit the Company's Careers Page: Most companies have a "Careers" or "Jobs" section on their website. Look for the job roles that interest you and ensure they align with your skills.
  2. Prepare Your Documents: Before applying, ensure you have your CV, cover letter, and any relevant documents (certificates, references) ready in the appropriate formats (PDF, Word).
  3. Complete the Application Form: Many companies ask you to fill out an online form. Be sure to answer all the questions truthfully and thoroughly. If there’s an option to upload your CV, make sure it is the correct version.
  4. Personalize Your Application: Just like with LinkedIn, tailor your cover letter to the specific role and company. Mention your skills and explain why you are a good fit for the role.
  5. Track Your Applications: Keep a record of the jobs you have applied to. You can use a spreadsheet or an app to track the positions and follow up when necessary.
