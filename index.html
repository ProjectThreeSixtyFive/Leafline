Executive Summary

South Africa is facing one of the most serious unemployment crises globally, with youth bearing the brunt of the economic instability. The official unemployment rate, according to Statistics South Africa, decreased slightly by 0.8 percentage points from 32.9% in Q3 2023 to 32.1% in Q4 2023. However, the expanded definition of unemployment — which includes discouraged work-seekers — shows a marginal decrease from 41.1% to 40.4%, highlighting a continued crisis of opportunity and systemic inequality.

While small improvements are noted, the overall levels remain dangerously high, particularly among young people aged 15–34, who make up a significant percentage of the unemployed population. This demographic continues to face major challenges, leading to increased social instability and mental health issues. In a third-world country like South Africa, the combination of unemployment and poverty is fueling hopelessness, eroding confidence, and damaging the sense of purpose in young people at an alarming rate.

Job instability and lack of opportunities are directly contributing to the mental health crisis. Individuals are losing hope in the traditional pathways of "study hard, find a job," because despite qualifications, the employment market cannot absorb the numbers. Desperation and uncertainty are becoming the norm, threatening both the social and economic fabric of the country.

LeafLine exists as a response to this reality. We aim to create bridges where walls exist — providing education, training, mentorship, and support structures that reignite the purpose, vision, and hope of South Africa’s youth. Our vision is to empower a generation that does not simply accept the status quo, but actively reshapes it for a better tomorrow.

Employment Insights

The latest figures indicate that the number of employed persons increased by 22,000 in Q4 2023 compared to Q3 2023. Despite this small growth, the unemployment rate remains critically high. Major sectors that recorded gains in employment include:

  • Community and Social Services: +170,000 jobs
  • Construction: +59,000 jobs
  • Trade: +50,000 jobs
  • Transport: +36,000 jobs
  • Finance: +10,000 jobs

Conversely, employment declines were reported in:

  • Manufacturing: -39,000 jobs
  • Private Households: -33,000 jobs
  • Mining: -32,000 jobs
  • Agriculture: -12,000 jobs
  • Utilities: -6,000 jobs

Many employed South Africans are also underemployed, desiring more working hours to meet basic living standards. Underemployment and job instability further deepen the mental and financial insecurity among families, especially youth entering a fragile economy.

These statistics reflect a country at a crossroads, where urgent and intentional action is needed. The figures serve not only as numbers but as a call to all stakeholders to innovate and uplift communities that have long been marginalized.
